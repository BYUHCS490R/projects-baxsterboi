Volleyball History

Volleyball was originally invented by William C. Morgan at Holyoke, Massachusetts. He was the physical education director at YMCA when inventing this sport. He was inspired by sport called badminton, that leading to his invention. The first Volleyball name is called Mintonette. He created many rules such as 1.98 meter tall net and many more.

Overtime, this sport famous become well-known and evolved overtime. For example, setting and spiking were introduced in the Phillipines in 1916. During World War I, American Forces introduced this sport with their allies along with other continents such as Europe and Asia.

First Volleyball tournament was held at the YMCA in 1922. This is when many interest increased among people in Volleyball in the United States. Volleyball then was proposed by the United States to be part of Olympic at 1924.
