Volleyball Origin

Volleyball was originally invented by William G, Morgan in Holyoke, Massachusetts in 1895. He was the physical eucation director at the YMCA at that time. He called it mintonette at that time because he derived the sport from Badminton. He wrote many first mintonette rules such as 6 ft 6 high net and many more.

Overtime, Mintonette evolved overtime kike changing rules and play style. For example. The game became exhibit at the International YMCA Training School. The name of the sport changed to Volleyball since then. In early 1900s, American Sports Publishing Company produced books that contained complete instructions and rules for the sport.

Although the rules and instructions of Volleyball has been completed, it still evolved for time with new play style. For example, Phillipines in 1916 invented set and spike which makes the sport more engaging and intense. After a few years, "three hits rule also was introduced.

During world war 1, the American Forces spread the Volleyball across many continents such as Europe, Asia and Russia. The sport is now well-known in those countries. Overtime, there are many variants of Volleyball have been created, which makes it more fun and engaging.
